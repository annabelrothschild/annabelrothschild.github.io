Hello! // Salut!

I'm a Junior at Wellesley College where I study Computer Science, Statistics, and French. I'm interested in anything that has to do with data - particularly those applications with social effects. I'm an Undergraduate Research Assistant at Wellesley's CRED Lab, run by Dr. Eni Mustafaraj, where we study how people evaluate credibility on the web and help them build the tools to do so. Currently, I'm part of a project dealing with the representation of local newspapers on Wikipedia, to help with the lateral reading process that prevents the spread of fake news. Previously, I worked on the Atlanta Map Room, led by Dr. Yanni Loukissas at Georgia Tech, to help contextualize personal, lived histories in Atlanta in the face of a changing city, especially in regards to gentrification. A video describing our work can be found here.

Besides programming, I spend my time gardening succulents, hiking, building my photography portfolio, and admiring the work of Hilma af Klint. Feel free to send me an email if you'd like to hear more (ar@annabelrothschild.space)
